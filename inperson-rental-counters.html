Frequently Asked Questions

What documents are required to rent a car at an in-person rental counter?
To rent a car, you typically need to present a valid drivers license, a credit card in the name of the person renting the vehicle, and sometimes additional identification such as a passport or proof of insurance. Some companies might also require you to be over a certain age, often 25 years old, although younger drivers may still rent with additional fees.
How can I ensure availability of a specific car model when using an in-person rental counter?
To ensure the availability of a specific car model, its best to reserve your vehicle in advance either online or by phone. While walk-ins are welcome at most in-person rental counters, they operate on a first-come-first-served basis and specific models cannot be guaranteed without prior reservation.
Are there any hidden fees I should be aware of when renting from an in-person rental counter?
Be aware that beyond the base rental rate, there could be additional charges such as taxes, airport surcharges if renting from an airport location, insurance waivers, fuel charges if you dont return the car with a full tank, late return fees, and charges for optional extras like GPS or child seats. Ask for a detailed breakdown of all costs before finalizing your rental agreement to avoid surprises.
