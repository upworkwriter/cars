Frequently Asked Questions

What insurance options are typically offered by car rental services?
Car rental services typically offer several types of insurance options which may include Collision Damage Waiver (CDW), Loss Damage Waiver (LDW), Personal Accident Insurance (PAI), Personal Effects Coverage (PEC), and Supplemental Liability Insurance (SLI). The specific offerings can vary by company and location.
Are the insurance options provided by car rental companies mandatory?
No, the insurance options offered by car rental companies are not mandatory. However, some form of insurance is highly recommended for financial protection in case of an accident or theft. You may decline the rental companys insurance if you have coverage through your personal auto insurance policy, credit card benefits, or a third-party insurer.
Can I use my own auto insurance for a rented vehicle instead of purchasing coverage from the rental agency?
Yes, in many cases you can use your own auto insurance policy to cover a rented vehicle. Before renting a car, contact your insurance provider to confirm that your policy extends to rental vehicles and understand what is covered. Keep in mind that any claims made may affect your personal premiums.
What does a Collision Damage Waiver or Loss Damage Waiver typically cover and exclude?
A Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) or Loss Damage Waiver (LDW) generally covers damage to the rented vehicle due to collision or theft. However, it often excludes damages resulting from negligent behavior such as off-road driving, speeding, driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol, or unauthorized drivers operating the vehicle. Its important to read the terms carefully for exclusions before deciding whether to accept or waive this option.
